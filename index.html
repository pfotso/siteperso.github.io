Research topics

  • Public policies in the marine environment.


    • The marine environment is subject to various uses, all of which are factors of pressure on ecosystems. While sectoral approaches to regulating these activities respond to limited environmental protection, the implementation of a comprehensive and integrated approach in public policies will help to define a strategic framework for environmental protection. This new perspective will make it possible to respond to issues such as improving existing legal rules but also to offer an ecological leaven in integrated public development policies.

  • Challenges in marine environment.

    Various phenomena such as diffuse contamination of the marine environment that have an impact on fisheries products, climate change, constitute an experimental field for environmental law. It is an opportunity to consider the prospects for a new evolution of current legislation in order to meet these challenges.

  • Protection of marine and coastal environment in new activities.

    Maritime activities law has focused on the regulation of traditional activities such as fishing, transport or navigation. Its scope is gradually expanding with the emergence of new activities, mainly in the field of Renewable Marine Energies (EMR). However, the rules applicable to MREs do not exist everywhere in the world. These activities, which aim to limit the exploitation of resources from fossil fuels, are new and attractive markets for investors. The link between the rules of the law of the sea, the protection of the marine environment and market law is an opportunity to be seized in the context of public policies.
